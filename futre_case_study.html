Project Overview

Modern students need comprehensive, transparent career information to make informed decisions about their futures. Traditional career assessments are outdated and fail to showcase the full spectrum of career possibilities, especially those that don't require a four-year degree. How might we create an engaging, data-rich platform that empowers students to explore 800+ careers and plan their unique pathways?

The Challenge

Students felt overwhelmed by career options and lacked detailed information to evaluate careers meaningfully. Existing tools were either too generic or too complex, failing to present data in digestible, scannable formats.

My Solution

I designed and built the UI/UX for Futre's career exploration platform, created a comprehensive data ecosystem for 2000+ careers, and developed an AI-powered career recommender. My work focused on making complex career data accessible, engaging, and actionable through intuitive interface design, systematic data architecture, and user-centered interactions.

Impact

The platform now serves students across multiple schools with 800+ careers, each enriched with salary data, growth projections, educational requirements, and personalized pathways. The AI recommender achieved 68% match satisfaction in pilot testing, while interface improvements increased task completion by 70% and activation by 19%.

"The platform's success is measured not just in numbers, but in the confidence students gain to explore their futures."

Research & Discovery

To design an effective career exploration platform, I needed to deeply understand how students think about their futures, what information they need, and what barriers prevent them from making confident career decisions.

RESEARCH APPROACH

01

USER DIVERSITY

>

Conducted and moderated 48 Zoom interviews with high school and college students to understand their career exploration behaviors, pain points, and information needs.

02

EDUCATOR INSIGHTS

Interviewed career counselors and educators to understand how they guide students and what resources they find most effective.

03

BEHAVIORAL ANALYSIS

Used GA4 and Looker to analyze user behavior patterns, identifying where students dropped off and what content they engaged with most.

KEY INSIGHTS

1. INFORMATION OVERLOAD VS. LACK OF DEPTH

Students felt overwhelmed by too many options but simultaneously lacked the detailed information needed to evaluate careers meaningfully. They wanted comprehensive data presented in digestible, scannable formats.

2. VISUAL ENGAGEMENT MATTERS

Career pages with generic stock photos or text-heavy layouts failed to capture student attention. Students responded positively to unique, vibrant visuals that helped them imagine themselves in different careers.

3. TRANSPARENCY BUILDS TRUST

Students wanted honest information about salaries, job outlook, required education, and daily work realities. Hiding or sugarcoating information reduced trust and engagement.

4. PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE REDUCES OVERWHELM

The tab navigation system (Discover, Explore, Plan) allowed students to dive deeper into career details at their own pace without feeling overwhelmed by information density.

5. TESTING REVEALS UNEXPECTED INSIGHTS

48 user interviews revealed that students wanted to save and compare multiple careers simultaneously—a feature that became central to the dashboard design.

6. AI RECOMMENDATIONS NEED EXPLANATION

Students trusted AI recommendations more when they understood why careers were suggested. Transparency in the matching algorithm increased satisfaction from 52% to 68%.

Information Architecture

01

TAB NAVIGATION SYSTEM

Organized career information into three progressive tabs: Discover (overview and salary), Explore (requirements and pathways), and Plan (personalized recommendations and next steps). This structure reduced cognitive load and allowed students to explore at their own pace.

02

DATA VISUALIZATION

Transformed complex data (salary ranges, job growth projections, education requirements) into scannable visual formats using charts, progress bars, and color-coded indicators.

03

COMPONENT LIBRARY

Built a scalable design system with reusable components (career cards, stat displays, pathway diagrams) that maintained consistency across 800+ pages while allowing for content flexibility.

04

ITERATIVE TESTING

Conducted multiple rounds of usability testing with students, incorporating feedback to refine navigation, improve readability, and enhance engagement.

FINAL UI DESIGN

The final design showcases a clean, modern interface that balances comprehensive data with visual appeal and usability.

CAREER OVERVIEW: DISCOVER TAB

The Discover tab provides an at-a-glance overview of each career, including salary ranges, job outlook, and a brief description. The dark theme with teal accents creates a modern, engaging aesthetic.

CAREER DETAILS: EXPLORE TAB

The Explore tab dives deeper into educational requirements, necessary skills, work environment, and daily responsibilities. Information is organized into scannable sections with clear headings.

EDUCATIONAL REQUIREMENTS: EXPLORE TAB

Detailed breakdown of education pathways, including degree requirements, certifications, and alternative routes. Visual indicators show the typical education level for each career.

WORKING KNOWLEDGE & ENVIRONMENT

Provides insights into the daily work environment, required skills, and knowledge areas. Helps students understand what it's really like to work in each career.

RELATED CAREERS EXPLORATION

Suggests related careers based on similar skills, education, or interests. Encourages students to explore multiple pathways and discover careers they might not have considered.

PERSONALIZED PLANNING: PLAN TAB

The Plan tab offers personalized next steps, including recommended courses, certifications, and action items. Students can save careers to their dashboard for comparison.

CAREER DASHBOARD & RECOMMENDATIONS

The main dashboard displays all saved careers with unique "vibe-coded" header images. Students can compare careers side-by-side and receive AI-powered recommendations.

DATA COLLECTION METHODOLOGY

01

SOURCE IDENTIFICATION

Identified credible sources for career data, including the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), O*NET, industry reports, and professional associations.

02

DATA EXTRACTION & STRUCTURING

Extracted relevant data for each career across 10+ categories: salary ranges, job outlook, educational requirements, skills, work environment, daily tasks, career pathways, related careers, certifications, and industry trends.

03

STANDARDIZATION & QUALITY ASSURANCE

Standardized data formats and terminology across all careers to ensure consistency. Implemented quality assurance processes to verify accuracy and completeness.

04

CONTINUOUS UPDATES

Established processes for regularly updating career data to reflect changing job markets, salary trends, and industry developments.

This comprehensive data ecosystem became the foundation of Futre's value proposition. By providing detailed, accurate, and up-to-date information for 2000+ careers, we empowered students to make informed decisions based on real-world data rather than assumptions or stereotypes.

"VIBE CODING" CAREER HEADER IMAGES

One of the most creative challenges was generating unique, engaging header images for each career. Rather than using generic stock photos, I "vibe coded" custom images using AI generation tools, ensuring each career had a distinctive, engaging visual identity that resonated with students.

THE CREATIVE PROCESS

01

CAREER ESSENCE CAPTURE

For each career, I identified its core essence, mood, and visual themes. For example, a geographer might suggest exploration, nature, and discovery, while a software engineer might evoke innovation, technology, and problem-solving.

02

PROMPT ENGINEERING

Crafted detailed AI prompts that captured the career's vibe, including setting, diverse representation, composition rules (person in environment, clear focal point), and mood descriptors.

03

GENERATION & CURATION

Generated multiple image variations for each career and curated the best ones based on visual appeal, diversity in representation, and alignment with the career's essence.

04

OPTIMIZATION & INTEGRATION

Optimized images for web performance (compression, responsive sizing) while maintaining visual quality. Integrated images into the design system with consistent aspect ratios, border radius, and overlay treatments.

WHY CUSTOM VISUALS MATTER

User testing revealed that students spent 40% more time on career pages with unique, high-quality header images compared to pages with generic stock photos. The custom visuals helped students imagine themselves in different careers and made the exploration experience more engaging and memorable.

"The images really help me picture myself in these careers. They feel real and inspiring, not like those boring stock photos."

— college sophomore, pilot testing participant

AI Career Recommender DESIGN

In addition to the career exploration interface, I designed and tested an AI-powered career recommendation system that matches students with careers based on their interests, skills, values, and goals.

DESIGN APPROACH

01

INPUT DESIGN

Created an engaging questionnaire that captured student interests, strengths, values, and constraints without feeling like a test. Used conversational language, visual answer options, and progress indicators to maintain engagement.

02

ALGORITHM COLLABORATION

Designed, specified, and tested an AI-powered career recommendation system in 5 weeks. Collaborated with engineering to define matching algorithms and user flows that achieved 68% satisfaction in pilot testing.

03

RESULTS PRESENTATION

Designed the results page to show match percentages, career highlights, and next steps. Included explanations of why careers were recommended to build trust and transparency.

04

PILOT TESTING

Conducted pilot testing with students to validate the recommendation quality. Achieved 68% match satisfaction, with students reporting that the recommendations were relevant and eye-opening.
