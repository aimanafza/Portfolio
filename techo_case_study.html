TECHO ARGENTINA

Designing TECHO's First Online Store

Product Design | E-Commerce | Non-Profit

üìã Project Overview

TECHO is a non-profit organization working to overcome poverty in Latin America through youth-led community development. I was brought on as a Product Designer to design their first-ever online store, enabling supporters to purchase merchandise and donate to the cause through a seamless digital experience.

The challenge was to create an e-commerce platform that balanced TECHO's mission-driven values with conversion optimization, making it easy for supporters to contribute while maintaining the organization's authentic, grassroots identity.

Role

Product Designer

Duration

üéØ The Challenge

TECHO had never had an online store before. All merchandise sales and donations were handled through in-person events or third-party platforms that took significant commission fees. The organization needed a custom e-commerce solution that would:

  • Reduce checkout friction (existing process had 9 steps)
  • Increase conversion rates for both merchandise and donations
  • Maintain TECHO's authentic, community-focused brand identity
  • Work seamlessly on mobile devices (primary user platform)
  • Integrate with existing payment systems in Argentina

üí° The Solution

I designed a streamlined e-commerce platform that reduced the checkout process from 9 steps to just 3, resulting in a 15% increase in conversion rates. The design prioritized mobile-first interactions, clear calls-to-action, and emotional storytelling that connected merchandise purchases to TECHO's mission.

Key features included one-click donation options, progress indicators during checkout, and impact statements showing how each purchase contributed to community development projects.
